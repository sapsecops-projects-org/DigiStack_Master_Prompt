# Curriculum Plan — Digistack Academy (Day-Wise, Sprint-Wise, Wave-Based)

Full-time pace. New weekly cadence locked in: **5 days study, Day 6 Weekly Revision, Day 7 Weekly LAB**, repeated twice, then a bonus **Production Simulation day** closes each 15-day cycle. Total: **465 calendar days** (~15.3 months) covering 310 content days — a floor, not a ceiling.

**Lab days alternate by design:** Week A's lab = *Basic Setup & Config* (rebuild/practice what was just taught, using `06_LAB_RECIPES.md`). Week B's lab = *Advanced Scenario* (a tiered challenge from `07_LAB_CHALLENGE_BANK.md` tied to that fortnight's content). Adjust this split anytime if you'd rather weight it differently.

**Part 1 is split into two Waves**, as requested: **Wave 1 — Build the Environment** (Foundations, Core ND Architecture, Install & Configuration) and **Wave 2 — Operate & Harden** (App Deployment, Security, Integration, Automation Basics).

## Pace Deviation Policy
If actual pace drifts from plan, apply this instead of deciding ad hoc:
- **1 phase behind, buffer sprint coming soon:** let the next Revision or Production Simulation day absorb the slip — no plan change needed
- **1 phase behind, no buffer coming up soon:** compress the *current* Part's remaining content days by merging adjacent low-risk topics. Never compress a Revision, Lab, or Production Simulation day — those are where real understanding gets tested
- **2+ phases behind:** stop and run the Periodic Gap-Check Prompt (see `11_SESSION_PROMPT_TEMPLATE.md`) early, out of cycle. Decide explicitly whether to (a) extend total program days, or (b) shrink/drop a lower-priority topic — the Advanced Architect Extension is the first candidate, since it's already flagged as optional/pullable. Log the decision in `04_PROGRESS_LOG.md`'s Key Decisions Log
- **Ahead of pace:** do not skip ahead into new content. Use the extra time for deeper documentation, extra Troubleshooting Playbook entries, or an unscheduled Advanced Lab from `07_LAB_CHALLENGE_BANK.md`

Rule of thumb: **never compress a Revision, Lab, or Production Simulation day to save time** — those are the highest-value parts of the whole program.

## Missed Study Day Policy (LOCKED)
Distinct from the Pace Deviation Policy above, which handles falling behind *while still actively working*. This handles gaps where nothing happens at all — illness, travel, work emergency, life event.

Because day numbers are relative, not calendar-bound (see Scheduling below), a missed day is never made up or skipped in content — you simply resume at the exact day you left off, whenever that is.

| Gap Length | Handling |
|---|---|
| **Short (1–2 days)** | No special handling. Next session's Morning Stand-up opens normally. |
| **Medium (3–7 days)** | Morning Stand-up includes a one-line in-fiction "welcome back" beat (e.g., a stand-in covered your tickets), then proceeds normally. Nothing is compressed to "catch up" — there's nothing to catch up on, since the day count never moved. |
| **Long (8+ days)** | Same as above, plus a brief 3–4 line context-rebuild pulled from `04_PROGRESS_LOG.md`'s last entries before resuming content. Logged as one line in `04_PROGRESS_LOG.md`'s Key Decisions Log (gap date range + resume day) — per Core Operating Principle #4, Lightweight Logging Only, in `01_MASTER_CONTEXT.md`. |

**Never applies to Revision/Lab/ProdSim days** — these aren't skipped or merged due to a gap; whichever day was next when you left is the day you resume on, consistent with the Pace Deviation Policy's "never compress" rule above.

**Relationship to Pace Deviation Policy:** that policy governs drift while actively working; this one governs zero-activity gaps. Both can apply in sequence — e.g., a long gap, followed by genuine pace drift once you're back and mid-topic.
